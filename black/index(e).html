What is black metal?

Black metal is an extreme subgenre of heavy metal, emerging in the mid-1980s. It is characterized by its Satanic-themed lyrics. It originated as a musical expression of anti-Christian movements proliferating across Europe, with the greatest impetus through the burning of numerous Christian churches.

Characteristics

Traditional black metal has very defined basic characteristics, which are: high-pitched guttural vocals commonly called shriek, fast guitars, dark atmosphere, and unusual harmonies and extremely fast rhythms. Most bands use a simple method in composing music, while others are more characterized by technique.

Timeline and Impact

It emerged in the 1980s with bands like Venom, Bathory, and Celtic Frost, but it solidified in the 1990s in Norway with bands like Mayhem, Burzum, Darkthrone, and Emperor. During this period, the genre was marked by controversies such as church burnings and murders. Over time, black metal evolved into various variants such as symphonic, atmospheric, and depressive black metal, expanding its sound without losing its extreme essence. Today, it remains an influential genre within metal.
